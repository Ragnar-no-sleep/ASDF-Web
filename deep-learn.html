⚙ The Burn Mechanism

When you "burn" tokens, you send them to an address with no private key. They're gone forever. No one can access them - ever.

Think of it like throwing money into a volcano. When supply decreases while demand stays constant, each remaining token becomes more scarce.

All burns are on-chain and publicly verifiable on Solscan.

🤖 The Daemon

The "Daemon" is an automated program running 24/7. It's not a person - it's deterministic code that:

  1. Detects accumulated fees in the treasury
  2. Collects and consolidates them
  3. Executes market buys for ASDF (via Jupiter)
  4. Sends purchased tokens to the burn address
  5. Logs everything on-chain

💰 External Revenue Source

Most deflationary tokens take a percentage from each transfer. ASDF is different.

External Revenue: Burns are funded by creator fees from Pump.fun trading - not from your transfers. This means burns happen regardless of ASDF-specific activity.
  • Creator fees fuel buybacks, not developer wallets
  • Selling generates burns too - sellers aren't enemies, they're fuel
  • Building apps? Your fees contribute to the burn

📈 K-Score: Measuring Conviction

The K-Score quantifies holder quality using a geometric mean formula. This ensures balanced quality - you can't compensate for weakness in one dimension with strength in another.

K = 100 × ³√(D × O × L)
Diamond × Organic × Longevity

The Three Dimensions

💎
D (Diamond)
Holder conviction depth - how strongly do holders believe?
🌱
O (Organic)
Natural distribution - is ownership decentralized or whale-dominated?
⏳
L (Longevity)
Time-tested survival - has the token survived market cycles?

🔒 Why Geometric Mean?

The geometric mean has a critical property: if any factor is zero, the entire result is zero.

Critical: If D=0 (no conviction), K=0. If O=0 (all whales), K=0. If L=0 (brand new), K=0. This enforces balanced quality - you can't fake your way to a high score.

Compare to arithmetic mean: a token with D=100, O=0, L=100 would get a score of 66.7. With geometric mean, it gets 0. No shortcuts.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is the burn address? Is it really inaccessible? ▼
The burn address is a Solana address with no known private key. Once tokens are sent there, they cannot be recovered by anyone - ever. You can verify this on Solscan by checking that the address has never signed any transactions.
How often do burns happen? ▼
Burns happen in "cycles" when accumulated fees reach a threshold. During high activity, burns can happen multiple times per day. You can monitor cycles via the /burns page.
Can the team "rug" or stop the burns? ▼
The daemon is automated code running independently. All transactions are transparent and on-chain. The code is open-source - anyone can audit it on GitHub.
What's the total supply and how much has been burned? ▼
Initial supply was 1 billion tokens. Approximately 8% has been burned. Check real-time stats at alonisthe.dev/burns.
What is the K-Score and why does it matter? ▼
The K-Score measures holder quality using geometric mean of Diamond (conviction), Organic (distribution), and Longevity (time). It prevents gaming because if any dimension is zero, the whole score is zero. High K-Score = healthy token.
Why does ASDF use the golden ratio (φ)? ▼
φ (1.618...) provides mathematical elegance and removes arbitrary decisions. Instead of "let's use 60%", we use 1/φ = 61.8%. It's found throughout nature and creates aesthetically balanced ratios. Every parameter in ASDF derives from φ.
Is this a good investment? ▼
We don't give financial advice. ASDF is a memecoin with experimental tokenomics. Do your own research, understand the risks, and never invest more than you can afford to lose.
How can I contribute? ▼
All code is open-source on GitHub! You can contribute code, build apps that integrate with the burn mechanism, or simply spread the word. Remember: BUILD > USE > HOLD - builders get φ² multiplier.
